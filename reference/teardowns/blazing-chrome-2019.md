## The 5 rubric axes

### 1. Sprite & animation quality — BAR: HIGH (≈5, the pinnacle-adjacent tier)
Heroes are ~14% of screen height with **crisp rim/outline contrast** that pops off a busy
background (tan-orange soldier on rust-red metal / on purple dusk). Every enemy is individually
detailed — the blue heavy mech (shot-00) has segmented armor plating + hydraulic joints, not a
color blob. The boss (boss-composition) is a huge **multi-part articulated** biomech beast with
per-segment shading. **OUR state (dim-1 4.0):** our hero/grunt/turret/Sentinel sprite set now
meets the indie bar (readable outline, run/leap/prone), but per-frame animation density and
multi-part boss articulation are the headroom to this tier. **Gap: animation density.**

### 2. Hit feedback & hit-stop — BAR: VERY HIGH (≈5)
Two grounded signatures: (a) **kills GIB** — shot-02's laser turns an enemy into flying brown
chunks + green *and* red splatter, multi-piece not a single puff; (b) explosions are a **dense
multi-puff billowing cluster** + a white **dash-ring** (firefight-explosion frame). Impacts read
instantly. **OUR state (dim-2 just verified → 4.0, ASSESS-20):** we landed a punchy single-burst
flash-pop + shock ring + fireball — reaches the competitor bar per-hit, but ours is ONE puff vs
BC's billowing cluster, and we do not gib. **Gap: multi-puff density + gib/splatter on death.**

### 3. Movement cadence — BAR: HIGH (now partially gradeable, was deferred)
The motion frames confirm what stills couldn't: co-op run-and-gun with grounded footfalls
(ground shadows track the feet, shot-00/02), a snappy melee scythe-arc that arcs *ahead* of the
hero, and a camera that holds both co-op players + a screen-wide laser in frame. Cadence reads
**fast and weighty** — no float. (Still ungradeable to the exact-frame: run-cycle frame COUNT
needs continuous footage, not trailer keyframes — carried as the residual under CAP-2.) **OUR
state (dim-3 3.5):** measured-exact 138 px/s + fixed-arc jump; arcade-plausible. In-band.

### 4. Weapon juice — BAR: VERY HIGH (≈5)
Projectiles are **art, not dots**: a screen-spanning laser with a white core and animated blue
crackle edges (shot-02); a golden chaingun muzzle-flash with tracer sparks + a gold energy bolt
from the mech (shot-00); a green melee slash arc. A 4-slot weapon-swap HUD telegraphs the
arsenal. **OUR state (dim-4 4.0):** full arcade M/S/L/F roster, each a distinct color+shape =
legible identity. **Gap: projectile glow/tracer polish** (ours are cleaner-edged squares vs BC's
crackling beam) — the SCORECARD's standing dim-4 headroom, now concretely anchored.

### 5. Enemy density & pacing — BAR: MEDIUM-HIGH
On-screen: 2 co-op heroes + 2–3 distinct enemies + projectiles + debris, never cluttered — the
**silhouette-contrast** keeps it readable at density. The climax is a **giant multi-part boss**
(boss-composition) that dwarfs the player. **OUR state (dim-5 4.0):** 23 spawns, 3–5 concurrent,
ground/fixed/aerial variety + a two-phase Sentinel boss. In-band on count; the boss is a single
articulated unit vs BC's screen-filling multi-part beast (BOSS-1 stretch).

## Net positioning (what this anchor tells the gate)
Blazing Chrome sits at the **top of OUR competitive tier** — it is the "match or exceed" target,
not the unreachable pinnacle (that's Metal Slug's hand-drawn density). OUR game is **at-bar on
identity, HUD legibility, weapon roster, and per-hit punch**, with three concrete, non-blocking
stretches to fully reach BC: **(a)** multi-puff explosion density + gib/splatter (dim-2), **(b)**
projectile glow/crackle (dim-4), **(c)** a multi-part boss set-piece (dim-1/5, BOSS-1). None are
regressions; all are the same headroom the SCORECARD already lists — now grounded frame-by-frame
against the specific title we most compare against.
